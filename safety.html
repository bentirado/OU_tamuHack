Your Safety Guide

Practical tips and information to help you feel prepared and secure during your flight.

Choosing Your Seat

While all airplane seats are safe, some passengers prefer certain locations for added comfort and peace of mind.

Over the Wing

Most stable area with least turbulence sensation

Exit Rows

Extra legroom and closest to exits

Front Section

Generally experiences less motion

Expert Tip

"Choose a seat over the wing if you're concerned about turbulence. This area acts like a fulcrum point, experiencing less movement during flight."

- Aviation Safety Expert

Pre-flight Preparation

Familiarize yourself with your airline's safety card
Note the locations of nearest exits
Keep essential medications in carry-on
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothes
Stay hydrated before and during flight

During Your Flight

Keep your seatbelt fastened when seated
Listen to crew instructions
Stay calm during turbulence - it's normal
Move occasionally to prevent stiffness
Ask crew if you need reassurance

Personal Comfort Items

Noise-Canceling Headphones

Reduce engine noise and create a calming environment

Travel Comfort Kit

Eye mask, neck pillow, and comfortable socks

Entertainment

Loaded tablet or books to stay distracted

Remember

Commercial aviation is one of the safest forms of travel. Every safety measure, procedure, and equipment on board has multiple backups. The crew is highly trained and ready to assist you at any time during your flight.
