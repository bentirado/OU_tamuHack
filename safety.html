Your Safety Guide

Practical tips and information to help you feel prepared and secure during your flight.

Turbulence

While turbulence can sound scary, it is a normal occurence and airlines are prepared for it. Since 2009, there have been only 184 injuries due to turbulence. Here are some guidelines to stay safe while traveling on a plane.

Keep your seatbelt buckled at all times.
Listen to the pilots and flight attendants.
Prevent inflight injuries by adhering to your airline's carry-on restrictions.

Passenger Safety Information

Review the passenger safety card before takeoff
Listen carefully to the safety briefing.
Be able to locate emergency exits both in front and behind you. Count the rows between you and the nearest front and rear exits.
Locate the flotation device.
Make a mental plan of action in case of emergency.

Evacuation Procedures

  • Leave your possesions behind.
  • Stay low.
  • Proceed to the nearest front or rear exit - count the rows between your seat and the exits.
  • Jump feet first onto evacuation slide. Don't sit down to slide. Place arms across your chest, elbows in, and legs and feet together. Remove high-heeled shoes.
  • Exit the aircraft and clear the area.
  • Never return to a burning aircraft.

Remember

Commercial aviation is one of the safest forms of travel. Every safety measure, procedure, and equipment on board has multiple backups. The crew is highly trained and ready to assist you at any time during your flight.
